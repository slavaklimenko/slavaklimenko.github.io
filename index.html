Tracing the matter between the stars and around galaxies.

Research

Gas and dust as tracers of galaxy evolution

I use background stars and quasars as bright beacons to measure material that is difficult to detect in emission, then combine those measurements with spatially resolved observations of the associated galaxies.

01

Molecular gas in the early Universe

I study H2, CO, and neutral carbon absorption in damped Lyα systems at z ≈ 2–3. Excitation modeling constrains gas temperature, density, ultraviolet radiation, chemical enrichment, and the cosmic microwave background temperature.

Related publications →
02

The baryon cycle around galaxies

By combining HST/COS quasar absorption spectra with SDSS/MaNGA integral-field maps, I connect cold circumgalactic gas to galaxy rotation, metallicity gradients, star formation, and the flow of material into and out of galaxies.

Current surveys →
03

Dust composition and extinction

I use JWST mid-infrared spectroscopy and ultraviolet-to-near-infrared data to measure extinction curves, the 2175 Å carbonaceous feature, and silicate and ice absorption in galaxies at z ≈ 0.1–1.3.

Projects & observing programs

Current and recent work

My research combines targeted observations, large archival samples, and collaborations across space- and ground-based facilities.

HST/COS + SDSS/MaNGACurrent

Cold circumgalactic gas around nearby galaxies

This survey measures H I and metal absorption around galaxies at z = 0.02–0.1 and compares it with resolved MaNGA maps of stellar and gas kinematics, star formation, and chemical abundance. A pilot sample showed co-rotation between the inner CGM and galaxy disks and metallicities consistent with disk gradients; the expanded sample targets roughly 50 systems within the virial radius.

  • HST/COS
  • MaNGA
  • CGM
  • Galaxy kinematics
  • Metallicity
JWST/MIRI MRSCycles 1 & 3

Dust, ice, and molecules in distant galaxies

Four JWST programs use background quasars at impact parameters of 1–20 kpc to probe dust in normal galaxies. The analysis combines MIRI spectroscopy with GALEX, optical spectroscopy, and near-infrared photometry to measure silicate and ice features, extinction curves, grain composition, structure, and size distribution.

  • JWST
  • Dust extinction
  • Silicates
  • Ice features
  • Quasar sightlines
Archival IFU surveyz ≈ 0.8–6

Host galaxies of strong neutral-gas absorbers

A several-hundred-system survey brings together KCWI and OSIRIS on Keck; MUSE and SINFONI on the VLT; Gemini/GMOS; ALMA; and JWST/NIRSpec and MIRI observations. The goal is to identify and characterize the galaxies associated with strong damped Lyα absorbers across cosmic time.

  • DLA hosts
  • Integral-field spectroscopy
  • Neutral gas
  • Galaxy evolution
VLT + Keck2026 programs

Multiwavelength extinction and precision molecular spectroscopy

Current observing programs include the VLT/X-shooter SUENO survey of unexplored extinction in normal galaxies and the Keck/NIRES PRISM survey of reddening in intervening silicate-dust systems. Related VLT/ESPRESSO work uses ultra-high-resolution molecular absorption to study isotopic abundances, chemical evolution, and fundamental cosmology at cosmic noon.

  • VLT/X-shooter
  • Keck/NIRES
  • VLT/ESPRESSO
  • Extinction
  • CO

Software

Open and reproducible analysis

I develop public Python tools for dust-extinction modeling and for deriving physical conditions in the interstellar medium. Research code and ongoing projects are available through GitHub.

Dust-extinction modeling

Tools for fitting ultraviolet-to-infrared extinction curves and comparing carbonaceous, silicate, and ice absorption signatures across galaxy environments.
